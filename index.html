All Recipes, No Life Stories

One of my biggest pet peeves is looking for recipes online. This isn't because of a lack of availability, since nearly anything can be found on Pinterest, but the quality is hit and miss. I usually have to thoroughly inspect recipes to see if they're worth making before I go out and buy ingredients. Most food blogs are also more blog than food and force you to scroll through a wall of text and excess images (often littered with intrusive advertisements and desperate pop-ups begging you to join the mailing list) just to get to the recipe.


I don't want to have to read through someone's life story just to see if a recipe is worth my time, and you shouldn't have to either!


I've gone through a lot of online recipes during my time experimenting in the kitchen and, as such, have the "keepers" saved to a Google doc so I can avoid the annoying food blogs and make changes to them as I tinker with them. I have a lot of family and personal recipes stored this way as well. The problem? The document is disorganized, full of copy-pasted fonts and styles, and is a hassle for family to go through when they just want the strawberry rhubarb crumble pie recipe that was a hit at Thanksgiving. As such, this is the beginning of a streamlined recipe website that's designed for use in a kitchen, not a library.
